# PDF Output Expectations

The generated PDF should resemble a raw Outlook export / print output rather than a heavily formatted report.

---

# What the PDF SHOULD Contain

## 1. Email Sections
Each email should appear as a separate section containing:
- From
- Sent
- To
- CC (if applicable)
- Subject
- Body

Example structure:

```text
EMAIL 1
From:
Sent:
To:
Subject:
Body

EMAIL 2
...
```

---

## 2. Preserved Reply Chains
Retain:
- Outlook reply history
- quoted emails
- nested conversation context
- forwarded content

Do NOT aggressively clean or rewrite conversation history.

---

## 3. Minimal Formatting
Use simple Outlook-style formatting only.

Include:
- email signatures
- legal disclaimers
- plain text formatting
- Outlook-style layout

Do NOT add:
- branded cover pages
- logos
- custom corporate styling
- marketing-style formatting

---

# What the PDF SHOULD NOT Contain

Do NOT add:
- dedicated title pages
- summary dashboards
- metadata tables
- communication analytics
- participant summaries
- custom navigation
- table of contents
- bookmarks

Do NOT attempt to:
- deduplicate quoted history
- rewrite emails
- summarize threads
- remove repeated signatures/disclaimers unless clearly broken

---

# Ordering

Preserve the original Outlook/export ordering if possible.

Do NOT enforce:
- oldest → newest
or
- newest → oldest

unless specifically requested later.

---

# Attachments

Attachment handling is optional.

If implemented:
- attachment filenames may be referenced
- embedding attachments is not required

---

# Overall Goal

The final PDF should behave like:

> A consolidated raw Outlook correspondence export for each SMSF/client

NOT:

> A polished client report or presentation package
